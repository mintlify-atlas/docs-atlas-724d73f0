## Overview

SANL's data analysis capabilities combine real-time data from Supabase with advanced AI insights from Google Gemini to help healthcare providers make evidence-based decisions. The platform transforms raw patient data into actionable intelligence for nutritional interventions.

<Note>
  All analytics are updated in real-time as new patient data is entered, ensuring you always have the most current insights available.
</Note>

## Analyzing Individual Patient Data

### Accessing Patient Analytics

Navigate to comprehensive patient data analysis:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Patient Profile">
    Search for and select the patient from your dashboard.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Navigate to Analytics Tab">
    Click the **Analytics** tab to access the patient's data visualization suite.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Analysis Type">
    Choose from available analysis views:
    
    - **Timeline**: Chronological view of all measurements and interventions
    - **Trends**: Statistical analysis of specific metrics over time
    - **Correlations**: Relationship analysis between different health metrics
    - **Predictions**: AI-powered forecasting of future outcomes
    - **Comparisons**: Benchmarking against clinical guidelines or population norms
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customize Date Range">
    Set the time period for analysis:
    
    - Last 7 days (recent changes)
    - Last 30 days (monthly progress)
    - Last 90 days (quarterly trends)
    - Last year (annual review)
    - Since plan start (intervention effectiveness)
    - Custom date range
  </Step>
</Steps>

  <Step title="Select Analysis Focus">

    - Compliance pattern analysis
    - Risk factor identification
    - Optimization recommendations
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review AI Report">
    The AI generates a comprehensive analysis including:
    
    **Summary Section:**
    - Key findings in plain language
    - Most significant changes or patterns
    - Areas of concern or celebration
    
    **Detailed Analysis:**
    - Statistical trends with significance testing
    - Correlation analysis between metrics
    - Comparison to expected outcomes
    - Evidence-based interpretation

    - Specific dietary modifications
    - Lifestyle intervention suggestions
    - Follow-up testing or monitoring recommendations

  <Step title="Implement or Refine">

    - Request alternative approaches

      All AI interactions are logged and saved to the patient record for future reference and continuity of care.

### Understanding AI Confidence Scores

The AI system provides confidence ratings for its recommendations:

- **High Confidence (85-100%)**: Strong evidence support, clear data patterns, well-established interventions
- **Moderate Confidence (60-84%)**: Good evidence but some uncertainty, multiple valid approaches, or individual variation expected
- **Low Confidence (below 60%)**: Limited data, unusual patient presentation, or conflicting evidence

<Tip>
  Use your clinical judgment alongside AI recommendations. High confidence scores don't replace professional decision-making, especially for complex cases.
</Tip>

### Pattern Recognition

Identify clinically significant patterns in patient data:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="chart-line-up" title="Positive Trends">
    **Indicators of Success:**
    
    - Consistent movement toward goals
    - Stable or improving biomarkers
    - High compliance correlation with improvements
    - Reduced variability in key metrics
    - Patient-reported satisfaction increases
    
    **Actions:**
    
    - Acknowledge and celebrate progress with patient
    - Continue current intervention strategy
    - Consider gradual reduction in monitoring frequency
    - Document successful approaches for similar future cases
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="minus" title="Plateaus">
    **Plateau Characteristics:**
    
    - Flat trend despite continued intervention
    - Sustained over 2-4 weeks
    - No obvious compliance issues
    - Patient frustration may increase
    
    **AI-Suggested Interventions:**
    
    1. Recalculate energy expenditure based on new body weight
    2. Adjust macronutrient distribution
    3. Implement diet breaks or refeeds (weight loss cases)
    4. Increase physical activity recommendations
    5. Screen for metabolic adaptation
    6. Assess for unidentified compliance barriers
    
    <Tip>
      Plateaus are normal physiological responses. Educate patients proactively about expecting them to reduce frustration when they occur.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="chart-line-down" title="Adverse Trends">
    **Warning Signs:**
    
    - Movement away from goals
    - Worsening biomarkers
    - Declining compliance
    - Increasing metric variability
    - Patient-reported difficulties
    
    **Investigation Protocol:**
    
    1. **Review compliance data**: Is the plan being followed?
    2. **Check for life changes**: New stressors, schedule changes, etc.
    3. **Medication review**: Any changes affecting metabolism or appetite?
    4. **Plan suitability**: Is it too restrictive or incompatible with lifestyle?
    5. **Medical factors**: Undiagnosed conditions or disease progression?
    
    **Response Actions:**
    
    - Schedule urgent follow-up consultation
    - Simplify plan if overwhelming
    - Address identified barriers
    - Consider medical referral if concerning health changes
    - Modify goals if initially unrealistic
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="wave-square" title="Cyclical Patterns">
    **Common Cycles:**
    
    - Weekly patterns (weekend vs. weekday behaviors)
    - Monthly patterns (hormonal influences)
    - Seasonal variations (holiday periods, weather changes)
    - Stress-related cycles (work schedules, academic calendars)
    
    **Optimization Strategies:**
    
    - Create pattern-specific plan variations
    - Anticipate and plan for challenging periods
    - Set realistic expectations during difficult cycles
    - Focus maintenance during predictable low-compliance periods
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Correlation Analysis

### Multi-Variable Relationships

Explore how different factors influence each other:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Variables">
    Choose two or more metrics to analyze:
    
    - Independent variable (e.g., dietary compliance)
    - Dependent variable (e.g., weight change)
    - Optional: Additional factors (sleep, stress, activity)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Generate Correlation Plot">
    The system creates:
    
    - Scatter plots showing relationship
    - Correlation coefficient (r value)
    - P-value for statistical significance
    - Regression line if appropriate
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Interpret Results">
    **Correlation Strength:**
    
    - Strong (|r| > 0.7): Clear relationship
    - Moderate (|r| = 0.4-0.7): Notable association
    - Weak (|r| < 0.4): Minimal relationship
    
    <Warning>
      Remember: Correlation does not imply causation. Use clinical judgment to interpret relationships.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Apply Insights">
    Use correlation findings to:
    
    - Identify most impactful interventions
    - Personalize which metrics to emphasize
    - Predict outcomes based on compliance patterns
    - Adjust monitoring frequency based on key drivers
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Data Quality and Validation

### Ensuring Accurate Analysis

Maintain data integrity for reliable insights:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="circle-check" title="Automatic Validation">
    The system flags potentially erroneous data:
    
    - Biologically implausible values (e.g., 500 lb weight loss in a week)
    - Inconsistent units or conversions
    - Duplicate entries
    - Missing timestamps
    - Outliers beyond expected ranges
    
    Review flagged items and correct or confirm as appropriate.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="user-check" title="Manual Quality Checks">
    Regular data audits to ensure accuracy:
    
    1. Review recently entered data for obvious errors
    2. Verify measurement techniques are consistent
    3. Confirm patient-entered data makes sense
    4. Check for systematic entry errors (e.g., always entering kg as lbs)
    5. Validate imported data from external sources
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="clock-rotate-left" title="Audit Trail">
    Complete logging of data changes:
    
    - Who entered or modified each data point
    - When changes were made
    - Original values before modification
    - Reason for change (if documented)
    
    Essential for both clinical quality and regulatory compliance.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="chart-simple" title="Charts not displaying or loading slowly">
    **Common causes:**
    
    - Large date range with many data points
    - Browser cache issues
    - Slow internet connection
    - Database query timeout
    
    **Solutions:**
    
    1. Narrow the date range to reduce data volume
    2. Clear browser cache and refresh
    3. Check network connection
    4. Use aggregate views instead of granular data
    5. Contact support if database performance is slow
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="triangle-exclamation" title="AI insights seem incorrect or irrelevant">
    **Potential issues:**
    
    - Insufficient patient data for reliable analysis
    - Incomplete patient profile information
    - Data quality issues skewing AI interpretation
    - Unusual patient case outside AI training scope
    
    **Actions:**
    
    1. Verify patient profile completeness
    2. Check for data entry errors in recent measurements
    3. Ensure adequate data history (minimum 2-4 weeks)
    4. Provide additional context in patient notes
    5. Request alternative AI analysis
    6. Rely on clinical judgment if AI continues to miss mark
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="file-slash" title="Report generation fails">
    **Troubleshooting steps:**
    
    1. Check that patient has data in selected date range
    2. Verify all required data fields are populated
    3. Try generating with fewer report sections
    4. Check browser console for error messages
    5. Attempt with different report template
    6. Contact support with error details if persists
    
    PDF generation (fpdf2) occasionally fails with very large datasets or special characters in notes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
