## Overview

SANL's nutrition planning system enables healthcare providers to create evidence-based, personalized dietary interventions. The platform combines clinical data from Supabase with AI-powered recommendations from Google Gemini to deliver tailored nutrition strategies.

<Note>
  Nutrition plans automatically consider patient allergies, medications, and medical conditions to ensure safe and effective dietary recommendations.
</Note>

## Creating a Nutrition Plan

Develop comprehensive nutrition plans tailored to individual patient needs:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Patient and Goal">
    Begin by choosing the patient and defining nutritional objectives:
    
    1. Navigate to the patient profile
    2. Click **Create Nutrition Plan**
    3. Select the primary goal:
       - Weight management (loss, gain, or maintenance)
       - Blood sugar control
       - Cardiovascular health
       - Sports nutrition
       - Disease-specific dietary management
       - General wellness
    
    <Tip>
      You can set multiple goals, but designate one as primary to focus the AI recommendations.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Patient Context">
    The system automatically displays relevant patient information:
    
    - Current measurements (weight, BMI, blood glucose, etc.)
    - Active medical conditions and medications
    - Known food allergies and intolerances
    - Previous nutrition plans and outcomes
    - Dietary preferences and restrictions
    
    Review this information to inform your plan decisions.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Nutritional Parameters">
    Define the core nutritional framework:
    
    **Daily Caloric Target**
    - The system suggests a target based on BMR and activity level
    - Adjust for specific weight goals (deficit or surplus)
    - Factor in metabolic conditions
    
    **Macronutrient Distribution**
    - Protein: Set percentage or grams per kg body weight
    - Carbohydrates: Adjust based on metabolic health
    - Fats: Balance for satiety and essential fatty acids
    
    **Micronutrient Focus**
    - Highlight specific vitamins or minerals to emphasize
    - Address deficiencies identified in lab work
    
    <Warning>
      Always cross-reference macronutrient recommendations with patient medications. For example, patients on warfarin require consistent vitamin K intake.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Generate AI Recommendations">
    Leverage Google Gemini AI for intelligent meal planning:
    
    1. Click **Get AI Recommendations**
    2. The system analyzes:
       - Patient health data and goals
       - Cultural and personal preferences
       - Seasonal food availability
       - Budget considerations
       - Cooking skill level
    3. Review the AI-generated meal suggestions
    4. Edit, accept, or request alternative recommendations
    
    The AI provides:
    - Complete meal plans with recipes
    - Portion sizes and preparation instructions
    - Nutritional breakdown per meal and daily totals
    - Shopping lists organized by category
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customize Meal Plans">
    Refine the nutrition plan to match patient preferences:
    
    - Swap individual meals using the meal library
    - Adjust portion sizes for specific items
    - Add or remove snacks based on schedule
    - Set meal timing recommendations
    - Include hydration goals
    
    <Tip>
      Use the **Save as Template** feature for plans you frequently prescribe to save time on future similar cases.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Implementation Guidance">
    Provide practical instructions for patients:
    
    - Phase-in approach for major dietary changes
    - Meal prep strategies and batch cooking tips
    - Restaurant and dining-out guidelines
    - Travel and special occasion recommendations
    - Troubleshooting common challenges
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review and Publish">
    Finalize the nutrition plan:
    
    1. Review the complete plan in preview mode
    2. Verify all nutritional targets are met
    3. Check for any conflicts with medical data
    4. Add follow-up schedule and monitoring parameters
    5. Click **Publish Plan**
    
    The system will:
    - Make the plan available in the patient portal
    - Send email notification to patient
    - Generate a PDF version for download (using fpdf2)
    - Schedule automatic check-in reminders
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Making Adjustments

Adapt nutrition plans based on patient progress and feedback:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="sliders" title="Modify Existing Plans">
    Update plans without creating entirely new versions:
    
    1. Open the active nutrition plan
    2. Click **Edit Plan**
    3. Make necessary adjustments:
       - Increase or decrease calories
       - Adjust macronutrient ratios
       - Swap problematic foods
       - Modify meal timing
    4. Add a modification note explaining the changes
    5. Save as a new version
    
    The system maintains version history so you can review the evolution of the plan over time.
    
    <Note>
      Patients receive automatic notifications when their plan is updated with a summary of key changes.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="lightbulb" title="Use AI Optimization">
    Let AI suggest plan improvements based on patient data:
    
    1. Click **Optimize Plan** from the active plan view
    2. The AI analyzes:
       - Recent patient measurements and progress
       - Compliance patterns and logged meals
       - Patient feedback and reported challenges
       - Latest research on the patient's condition
    3. Review AI suggestions with rationale
    4. Accept, modify, or decline recommendations
    
    This feature uses Google Gemini to provide evidence-based optimization strategies.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="rotate" title="Create Plan Variations">
    Develop alternative versions for different scenarios:
    
    - **Weekday vs. Weekend**: Different plans for varying schedules
    - **Seasonal Variations**: Adjust for food availability
    - **Budget Tiers**: Options at different price points
    - **Preparation Time**: Quick meals vs. elaborate cooking
    
    Store all variations in the plan library for easy patient access.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="robot" title="AI recommendations seem inappropriate">
    **Possible causes:**
    
    - Incomplete or inaccurate patient data
    - Conflicting goals or parameters
    - Unusual dietary restrictions combination
    
    **Solutions:**
    
    1. Review patient profile for data accuracy
    2. Clarify primary vs. secondary goals
    3. Add context notes to guide AI reasoning
    4. Request alternative recommendations
    5. Manually create plan if AI continues to miss the mark
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="ban" title="Patient reports plan is too restrictive">
    **Assessment:**
    
    - Review compliance data to identify patterns
    - Schedule consultation to discuss barriers
    - Identify which specific recommendations are problematic
    
    **Adjustments:**
    
    - Increase caloric allowance if too low
    - Add more variety in meal options
    - Build in planned flexibility (80/20 approach)
    - Adjust meal timing to fit lifestyle better
    - Phase in changes more gradually
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion icon="chart-line-down" title="Patient not seeing expected results">
    **Investigation steps:**
    
    1. Verify actual compliance vs. reported compliance
    2. Check for medication changes affecting metabolism
    3. Review accuracy of food logging
    4. Assess for medical conditions affecting progress
    5. Calculate actual vs. theoretical energy expenditure
    
    **Potential adjustments:**
    
    - Recalculate nutritional targets based on actual data
    - Add metabolic testing if available
    - Adjust expectations based on individual metabolic rate
    - Consider referral to physician if medical issue suspected
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
